# NED University — MS Data Science (MSDS) Entry Test Overview

## 1. Topic breakdown (as given by your friend / prior test-takers)

| Topic | Approx. Questions | File |
|---|---|---|
| Statistics & Probability (mostly theory) | ~30 | `01` |
| Probability (numerical: simple & conditional) | ~8–10 (counted within the 30) | `02` |
| Computer Networking (IPv4, topologies) | ~5 | `03` |
| Object-Oriented Programming | ~5 | `04` |
| Software Development Models (Waterfall, Agile, Scrum...) | ~5 | `05` |
| Design & Analysis of Algorithms (complexity) | ~5–10 | `06` |
| Computer Science General Knowledge (e.g. "who invented the internet") | ~5 | `07` |

**Total: roughly 55–65 MCQs.** This means **Statistics & Probability alone is worth more than half the entire test** — it is by far your highest-priority subject.

## 2. General NED postgraduate test facts (confirmed from public sources — verify the exact figures for MSDS specifically on NED's official admission page before test day)

- Typical duration: around **120 minutes**.
- **No negative marking** — unlike FAST, wrong answers do NOT cost you marks at NED.
- A typical qualifying threshold is **50% marks** to be eligible — the test is a gate, not a fine-grained ranking tool the way FAST's merit-weighted score is, though your actual score may still matter for final merit ordering, so don't aim for just-barely-50%.
- Official department references: NED CSIT Department — admissions page (`cct.neduet.edu.pk/admission_ms`) and MSDS course list (`cct.neduet.edu.pk/msds_courses`).
- ⚠️ **Confirm your exact test date, duration, and passing percentage directly with NED's CSIT department or your admission portal** — these specifics can change year to year and weren't independently confirmable for the MSDS test specifically during research.

## 3. The single most important strategic difference from FAST

**FAST has negative marking. NED does not (based on available info).** That means:
- At FAST: only answer if you can eliminate ≥2 options.
- At NED: **attempt every single question, even pure guesses** — leaving a question blank only at NED costs you a free 25% chance (on a 4-option MCQ) for literally zero downside. Never leave a NED MCQ blank.

## 4. Priority order (by marks-per-minute-of-study)

1. **Statistics & Probability Theory** (file `01`) — biggest single block, ~20 questions worth.
2. **Probability Numericals** (file `02`) — same subject, ~8–10 questions, needs actual calculation practice not just theory recall.
3. **Design & Analysis of Algorithms** (file `06`) — 5–10 questions, and this overlaps directly with FAST's Specialization Area, so it's double-value study.
4. **Object-Oriented Programming** (file `04`) — 5 questions, also overlaps with FAST.
5. **Computer Networking** (file `03`) — 5 questions, IPv4 + topologies, fairly quick to learn cold facts for.
6. **Software Development Models** (file `05`) — 5 questions, mostly definitions/comparisons, fast to memorize.
7. **CS General Knowledge** (file `07`) — 5 questions, lowest study cost (pure facts), good for a final quick-hit review the night before.
