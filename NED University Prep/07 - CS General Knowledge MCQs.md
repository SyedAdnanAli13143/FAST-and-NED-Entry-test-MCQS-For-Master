# Computer Science General Knowledge (~5 Questions)

Pure recall — cheapest marks on the whole test in terms of study time. Do this file last, as a final quick-hit review.

**Q1.** Who is credited as a "father of the Internet" for co-developing the TCP/IP protocol?
A) Tim Berners-Lee  B) Vint Cerf  C) Bill Gates  D) Alan Turing
**Answer: B) Vint Cerf** (along with Bob Kahn)

**Q2.** Who invented the **World Wide Web** (a service that runs on top of the Internet)?
A) Vint Cerf  B) Tim Berners-Lee  C) Steve Jobs  D) Larry Page
**Answer: B) Tim Berners-Lee** (1989–1991)
> ⚠️ Common trap: "the Internet" and "the World Wide Web" are **not the same thing**. The Internet is the underlying network (older, from the 1960s–70s); the Web is a service/application built on top of it (created later, in 1989–91).

**Q3.** The earliest version of the Internet, developed in the late 1960s by the US Department of Defense, was called:
A) ARPANET  B) ETHERNET  C) INTERNET2  D) WWWNET
**Answer: A) ARPANET**

**Q4.** Who is considered the "father of the computer" for designing the Analytical Engine?
A) Alan Turing  B) Charles Babbage  C) John von Neumann  D) Dennis Ritchie
**Answer: B) Charles Babbage**

**Q5.** Who is regarded as the world's **first computer programmer**?
A) Ada Lovelace  B) Grace Hopper  C) Margaret Hamilton  D) Marie Curie
**Answer: A) Ada Lovelace**

**Q6.** Who proposed the "Turing Test" and is regarded as a father of theoretical computer science and AI?
A) John McCarthy  B) Alan Turing  C) Claude Shannon  D) Bill Gates
**Answer: B) Alan Turing**

**Q7.** The term "**Artificial Intelligence**" was first coined by:
A) Alan Turing  B) John McCarthy  C) Marvin Minsky  D) Geoffrey Hinton
**Answer: B) John McCarthy** (1956, Dartmouth Conference)

**Q8.** Who created the **C** programming language?
A) James Gosling  B) Dennis Ritchie  C) Guido van Rossum  D) Bjarne Stroustrup
**Answer: B) Dennis Ritchie**

**Q9.** Who created the **Python** programming language?
A) Guido van Rossum  B) Dennis Ritchie  C) James Gosling  D) Linus Torvalds
**Answer: A) Guido van Rossum**

**Q10.** Who created the **Java** programming language?
A) James Gosling  B) Bjarne Stroustrup  C) Dennis Ritchie  D) Yukihiro Matsumoto
**Answer: A) James Gosling**

**Q11.** Who created **C++** as an extension of C?
A) Dennis Ritchie  B) Bjarne Stroustrup  C) James Gosling  D) Guido van Rossum
**Answer: B) Bjarne Stroustrup**

**Q12.** Microsoft was co-founded by Bill Gates and:
A) Steve Jobs  B) Paul Allen  C) Larry Page  D) Steve Wozniak
**Answer: B) Paul Allen**

**Q13.** Apple was co-founded by Steve Jobs and:
A) Bill Gates  B) Steve Wozniak  C) Larry Page  D) Jeff Bezos
**Answer: B) Steve Wozniak**

**Q14.** Google was co-founded by Larry Page and:
A) Sergey Brin  B) Mark Zuckerberg  C) Elon Musk  D) Jeff Bezos
**Answer: A) Sergey Brin**

**Q15.** The Linux operating system **kernel** was created by:
A) Linus Torvalds  B) Dennis Ritchie  C) Richard Stallman  D) Bill Gates
**Answer: A) Linus Torvalds**

**Q16.** CPU stands for:
A) Computer Personal Unit  B) Central Processing Unit  C) Central Program Utility  D) Core Processing Unit
**Answer: B)**

**Q17.** RAM stands for:
A) Random Access Memory  B) Read Access Memory  C) Rapid Access Memory  D) Run Access Memory
**Answer: A)**

**Q18.** Which is considered the first general-purpose electronic computer, built in the 1940s?
A) IBM PC  B) ENIAC  C) Apple I  D) UNIVAC
**Answer: B) ENIAC**

**Q19.** HTTP stands for:
A) HyperText Transfer Protocol  B) High Transfer Text Protocol  C) HyperText Translation Process  D) Host Transfer Text Protocol
**Answer: A)**

**Q20.** Who developed "Information Theory," foundational to all modern digital communication?
A) Claude Shannon  B) Alan Turing  C) John von Neumann  D) Norbert Wiener
**Answer: A) Claude Shannon**
