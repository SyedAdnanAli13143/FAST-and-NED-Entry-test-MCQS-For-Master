# Analytical Skills & IQ (30% of your score) — The "Symbols, What Next" Section

This is the section you remember as "math question like symbols, what next." It is **not** advanced math — it's pattern recognition. No formula to memorize, just a skill you sharpen by doing many of these. Every question below is solved in plain English right after it. Cover the answer with your hand, solve it, then check.

There are 8 question types here. Work through all of them at least once, then redo the ones you got wrong.

---

## A. Symbol-Coded Arithmetic ("if + means ×...")

**This is almost certainly what you remember as "math like symbols."** A symbol or letter secretly stands for a different operation. Rule: replace every symbol with what it really means, *then* solve using normal order of operations (multiply/divide before add/subtract, left to right).

**Q1.** If `+` means `×`, `−` means `÷`, `×` means `+`, `÷` means `−`, find: `6 + 3 − 9 × 2 ÷ 4`
A) 0  B) 2  C) 4  D) −2
**Answer: A) 0** — Rewrite: 6×3÷9+2−4 → 18÷9=2 → 2+2−4 = **0**

**Q2.** If `A` means `+`, `B` means `−`, `C` means `×`, `D` means `÷`, find: `20 D 4 C 3 A 5 B 2`
A) 12  B) 18  C) 20  D) 15
**Answer: B) 18** — Rewrite: 20÷4×3+5−2 → 5×3=15 → 15+5−2 = **18**

**Q3.** If `@` means "add 5 to", `#` means "multiply by 2", `$` means "subtract 3 from": Start with 4, apply `@`, then `#`, then `$`. What's the result?
A) 9  B) 12  C) 15  D) 18
**Answer: C) 15** — 4 →(@)→ 9 →(#)→ 18 →($)→ **15**

**Q4.** If `P` means `÷`, `Q` means `×`, `R` means `+`, `S` means `−`, find: `18 Q 2 P 4 R 6 S 3`
A) 9  B) 12  C) 15  D) 18
**Answer: B) 12** — Rewrite: 18×2÷4+6−3 → 36÷4=9 → 9+6−3 = **12**

**Q5.** If `+` means `−`, `−` means `×`, `×` means `÷`, `÷` means `+`, find: `10 + 2 − 3 × 6 ÷ 2`
A) 9  B) 10  C) 11  D) 12
**Answer: C) 11** — Rewrite: 10−2×3÷6+2 → 2×3=6, 6÷6=1 → 10−1+2 = **11**

**Q6.** If `+` means `×`, `×` means `−`, `−` means `÷`, `÷` means `+`, find: `24 ÷ 4 + 3 − 2 × 5`
A) 20  B) 22  C) 25  D) 27
**Answer: C) 25** — Rewrite: 24+4×3÷2−5 → 4×3=12, 12÷2=6 → 24+6−5 = **25**

**Q7.** If `+` means `×`, `×` means `+`, `−` means `÷`, `÷` means `−`, find: `12 − 3 × 4 + 8 ÷ 2`
A) 30  B) 32  C) 34  D) 36
**Answer: C) 34** — Rewrite: 12÷3+4×8−2 → 12÷3=4, 4×8=32 → 4+32−2 = **34**

**Q8.** If `A` means `+`, `B` means `−`, `C` means `×`, `D` means `÷`, find: `50 D 5 A 10 C 2 B 5`
A) 20  B) 22  C) 25  D) 28
**Answer: C) 25** — Rewrite: 50÷5+10×2−5 → 10+20−5 = **25**

**Golden rule for the real test:** write the substitution table on scratch paper FIRST (e.g. "+ = ×, − = ÷..."), rewrite the whole expression once, then calculate. Most mistakes happen from mixing up symbols mid-calculation.

## B. Number Series — find the next number

Look for: constant difference, constant ratio, increasing/decreasing differences, or alternating pattern.

**Q1.** 2, 4, 8, 16, 32, ?
A) 48  B) 56  C) 64  D) 128
**Answer: C) 64** — each number ×2

**Q2.** 1, 4, 9, 16, 25, ?
A) 30  B) 36  C) 32  D) 49
**Answer: B) 36** — perfect squares: 1²,2²,3²,4²,5²,**6²=36**

**Q3.** 3, 6, 11, 18, 27, ?
A) 36  B) 38  C) 40  D) 42
**Answer: B) 38** — differences: 3,5,7,9 (each +2) → next difference 11 → 27+11=**38**

**Q4.** 100, 90, 81, 73, 66, ?
A) 58  B) 60  C) 62  D) 64
**Answer: B) 60** — differences: −10,−9,−8,−7 → next −6 → 66−6=**60**

**Q5.** 2, 3, 5, 8, 13, 21, ?
A) 29  B) 32  C) 34  D) 36
**Answer: C) 34** — each number = sum of the two before it (Fibonacci pattern): 13+21=**34**

**Q6.** 5, 11, 23, 47, ?
A) 90  B) 93  C) 95  D) 99
**Answer: C) 95** — rule: ×2, then +1 → 47×2+1=**95**

**Q7.** 7, 9, 13, 19, 27, ?
A) 33  B) 35  C) 37  D) 39
**Answer: C) 37** — differences: 2,4,6,8 (each +2) → next difference 10 → 27+10=**37**

**Q8.** 81, 27, 9, 3, ?
A) 0  B) 1  C) 1.5  D) 2
**Answer: B) 1** — each number ÷3

## C. Letter Series — find the next letter(s)

Tip: write A–Z with position numbers (A=1, B=2 ... Z=26) on scratch paper. Letter puzzles are number puzzles in disguise.

**Q1.** A, C, E, G, ?
A) H  B) I  C) J  D) K
**Answer: B) I** — skip one letter each time (+2): G→**I**

**Q2.** B, D, G, K, ?
A) N  B) O  C) P  D) Q
**Answer: C) P** — gaps increase: +2,+3,+4 → next +5 → K(11)+5=16=**P**

**Q3.** Z, X, V, T, ?
A) Q  B) R  C) S  D) U
**Answer: B) R** — going backward by 2 each time: T(20)−2=18=**R**

**Q4.** A, D, I, P, ?
A) U  B) W  C) X  D) Y
**Answer: D) Y** — positions are perfect squares: A=1²(1), D=2²(4), I=3²(9), P=4²(16), next = 5²(25)=**Y**

**Q5.** AB, DE, GH, JK, ?
A) LM  B) MN  C) NO  D) OP
**Answer: B) MN** — first letter of each pair jumps +3 (A,D,G,J,**M**) and second letter is always next in alphabet → **MN**

**Q6.** J, L, N, P, ?
A) Q  B) R  C) S  D) T
**Answer: B) R** — +2 each time: P(16)+2=18=**R**

**Q7.** Y, W, U, S, ?
A) P  B) Q  C) R  D) T
**Answer: B) Q** — −2 each time: S(19)−2=17=**Q**

**Q8.** A, B, D, G, K, ?
A) N  B) O  C) P  D) Q
**Answer: C) P** — gaps increase by 1 each time: +1,+2,+3,+4 → next +5 → K(11)+5=16=**P**

## D. Coding-Decoding — word/letter substitution

A word is written in a secret code. Find the rule (usually: shift every letter forward/backward by a fixed amount, or reverse the word), then apply the *same* rule to a new word.

**Q1.** If `BOOK` is written as `CPPL`, how is `WORD` written?
A) XPSE  B) XPRE  C) XQSE  D) YPSE
**Answer: A) XPSE** — every letter shifts **+1**: W→X, O→P, R→S, D→E

**Q2.** If `TABLE` is written as `UBCMF`, how is `CHAIR` written?
A) DIBJS  B) DIBIS  C) DHBJS  D) EIBJS
**Answer: A) DIBJS** — every letter shifts +1: C→D, H→I, A→B, I→J, R→S

**Q3.** If `FRIEND` is written as `DNEIRF`, how is `CANDLE` written?
A) ELDNAC  B) EDLNAC  C) ELDNCA  D) LEDNAC
**Answer: A) ELDNAC** — the rule is simply **reverse the word**: CANDLE reversed = **ELDNAC**

**Q4.** If `PEN` is coded as `QFO`, what is `INK` coded as?
A) JOL  B) JNL  C) JOM  D) KOL
**Answer: A) JOL** — shift +1: I→J, N→O, K→L

**Q5.** If A=1, B=2 ... Z=26, and a code adds 2 to each letter's number, how is `CAT` coded (as letters)?
A) EDV  B) ECV  C) EBW  D) DCV
**Answer: B) ECV** — C(3)+2=5=E, A(1)+2=3=C, T(20)+2=22=V

**Q6.** If `SUN` is written as `TVO`, how is `RAIN` written?
A) SBJO  B) SBIO  C) SAJO  D) TBJO
**Answer: A) SBJO** — shift +1: R→S, A→B, I→J, N→O

**Q7.** If `TEACHER` is written as `VGCEJGT`, how would `STUDENT` be written?
A) UVWFGPV  B) UVWFGOV  C) UWVFGPV  D) UVVFGPV
**Answer: A) UVWFGPV** — every letter shifts **+2**: S→U, T→V, U→W, D→F, E→G, N→P, T→V

**Q8.** If `CHAIR` is written as `BGZHQ` (each letter shifts −1, wrapping A→Z), how is `HOUSE` written?
A) GNTRD  B) GMTRD  C) GNSRD  D) FNTRD
**Answer: A) GNTRD** — shift −1: H→G, O→N, U→T, S→R, E→D

## E. Blood Relations

Draw a quick family tree on scratch paper the moment you see one of these — don't try to hold it in your head.

**Q1.** Pointing to a photograph, a man says, "She is the daughter of my grandfather's only son." How is the girl related to the man?
A) Mother  B) Sister  C) Cousin  D) Aunt
**Answer: B) Sister** — "Grandfather's only son" = the man's own father (since he has no brothers). So the girl is his father's daughter = his **sister**.

**Q2.** A is the father of B. B is the sister of C (C is male). How is C related to A?
A) Brother  B) Son  C) Nephew  D) Father
**Answer: B) Son** — A is the father of B, and C is B's sibling, so C is also A's child → **Son**

**Q3.** Sara is an only child. Pointing to a boy, she says, "He is the son of my mother's only daughter." How is the boy related to Sara?
A) Nephew  B) Brother  C) Son  D) Cousin
**Answer: C) Son** — "Mother's only daughter," and Sara is an only child, means that daughter is Sara herself → the boy is **Sara's own son**.

**Q4.** P is Q's brother. R is Q's mother. S is R's father. How is P related to S?
A) Son  B) Grandson  C) Nephew  D) Brother
**Answer: B) Grandson** — P and Q share the same mother R, so P is R's son too. S is R's father, so S is P's grandfather → P is S's **grandson**.

**Q5.** A woman, who is an only child, says about a man, "He is the only son of my father's only daughter." How is the man related to the woman?
A) Brother  B) Nephew  C) Son  D) Cousin
**Answer: C) Son** — "My father's only daughter" = the woman herself → the man is the woman's own **son**.

**Q6.** A's mother is the sister of B (B is male), and B has no other siblings. How is B related to A?
A) Father  B) Maternal uncle  C) Grandfather  D) Cousin
**Answer: B) Maternal uncle** — B is A's mother's brother → B is A's **maternal uncle** (mamu).

## F. Syllogisms / Logical Deduction

You're given 2 statements (assume both are true). Decide whether the conclusion **definitely follows**, or **cannot be determined**. The trap is always the same: don't add outside assumptions — judge only what is logically forced.

**Q1.** All cats are animals. All animals need food. Conclusion: *All cats need food.*
A) Definitely true  B) Cannot be determined
**Answer: A) Definitely true** — simple valid chain: cats→animals→need food.

**Q2.** All roses are flowers. Some flowers fade quickly. Conclusion: *Some roses fade quickly.*
A) Definitely true  B) Cannot be determined
**Answer: B) Cannot be determined** — the "some flowers" that fade quickly are not guaranteed to include roses. Classic trap.

**Q3.** No reptiles are mammals. All snakes are reptiles. Conclusion: *No snakes are mammals.*
A) Definitely true  B) Cannot be determined
**Answer: A) Definitely true** — valid chain.

**Q4.** Some doctors are teachers. All teachers are educated. Conclusion: *Some doctors are educated.*
A) Definitely true  B) Cannot be determined
**Answer: A) Definitely true** — those doctors who are teachers must be educated, so some doctors are educated.

**Q5.** If it rains, the ground gets wet. The ground is wet. Conclusion: *It rained.*
A) Definitely true  B) Cannot be determined
**Answer: B) Cannot be determined** — the ground could be wet for other reasons (someone washed it). Never reverse an "if-then."

**Q6.** If you study, you will pass. You did not study. Conclusion: *You will not pass.*
A) Definitely true  B) Cannot be determined
**Answer: B) Cannot be determined** — not studying doesn't guarantee failure; you might pass anyway. Don't deny-the-antecedent.

**Q7.** All squares are rectangles. All rectangles have four sides. Conclusion: *All squares have four sides.*
A) Definitely true  B) Cannot be determined
**Answer: A) Definitely true** — valid chain.

**Q8.** No fish can fly. All sharks are fish. Conclusion: *No sharks can fly.*
A) Definitely true  B) Cannot be determined
**Answer: A) Definitely true** — valid chain.

## G. Analogies (word pairs & number pairs)

**Q1.** Pen : Write :: Knife : ?
A) Sharp  B) Cut  C) Kitchen  D) Metal
**Answer: B) Cut**

**Q2.** Doctor : Hospital :: Teacher : ?
A) Book  B) Student  C) School  D) Class
**Answer: C) School**

**Q3.** Bird : Nest :: Bee : ?
A) Flower  B) Honey  C) Hive  D) Garden
**Answer: C) Hive**

**Q4.** Author : Book :: Sculptor : ?
A) Chisel  B) Statue  C) Marble  D) Museum
**Answer: B) Statue**

**Q5.** 4 : 16 :: 5 : ?
A) 20  B) 24  C) 25  D) 30
**Answer: C) 25** — relationship is "square of the number" (4²=16, 5²=25)

**Q6.** 6 : 36 :: 9 : ?
A) 72  B) 81  C) 90  D) 99
**Answer: B) 81** — squares again (9²=81)

**Q7.** Hot : Cold :: Day : ?
A) Sun  B) Night  C) Light  D) Time
**Answer: B) Night**

**Q8.** Fish : Water :: Bird : ?
A) Tree  B) Air  C) Nest  D) Wing
**Answer: B) Air**

## H. Directions & Distance

Sketch a quick compass (N up, E right) on scratch paper — don't try to visualize turns in your head.

**Q1.** Ali walks 5 km North, then turns right and walks 3 km. Which direction is he now facing?
A) North  B) South  C) East  D) West
**Answer: C) East** — facing North, turning right (clockwise) → faces East.

**Q2.** From point X, a man walks 10m East, then 10m South, then 10m West. How far and in which direction is he from X?
A) 10m South  B) 10m East  C) 20m South  D) 0m (back at X)
**Answer: A) 10m South** — the East and West legs cancel out exactly; only the 10m South displacement remains.

**Q3.** Facing North, you turn 90° clockwise, then 180°. Which direction do you face now?
A) North  B) South  C) East  D) West
**Answer: D) West** — North → (90° CW) → East → (180°) → West.

**Q4.** A man walks 4 km East then 3 km North. What is his straight-line distance from the start?
A) 4 km  B) 5 km  C) 6 km  D) 7 km
**Answer: B) 5 km** — right triangle, legs 4 and 3: √(4²+3²) = √25 = **5**

**Q5.** If East becomes North, North becomes West, West becomes South, and South becomes East — what does North-East become?
A) North-West  B) South-East  C) South-West  D) North
**Answer: A) North-West** — the whole compass is rotated 90° the same way (each direction shifts one step counter-clockwise: E→N, N→W, W→S, S→E); applying the same shift, North-East → **North-West**.

**Q6.** A man walks 6 km North, then 8 km East. How far is he from his starting point?
A) 8 km  B) 10 km  C) 12 km  D) 14 km
**Answer: B) 10 km** — √(6²+8²) = √100 = **10**

## I. Ranking & Ordering

**Q1.** In a row, Asad is 7th from the left and 12th from the right. How many children are in the row?
A) 17  B) 18  C) 19  D) 20
**Answer: B) 18** — total = (position from left) + (position from right) − 1 = 7+12−1 = **18**

**Q2.** Sana is taller than Ayesha but shorter than Bisma. Who is the tallest of the three?
A) Sana  B) Ayesha  C) Bisma  D) Cannot be determined
**Answer: C) Bisma**

**Q3.** In a class of 30 students, Hamza ranks 12th from the top. What is his rank from the bottom?
A) 18  B) 19  C) 20  D) 21
**Answer: B) 19** — rank from bottom = total − rank from top + 1 = 30−12+1 = **19**

**Q4.** Bilal is 5th from the front of a queue, and there are 9 people behind him. How many people are in the queue in total?
A) 13  B) 14  C) 15  D) 16
**Answer: B) 14** — 4 people in front + Bilal himself + 9 behind = 4+1+9 = **14**

**Q5.** Box P is above Box Q. Box Q is above Box R. Box S is below Box R. Box T is above Box P. List the boxes from top to bottom.
A) T,P,Q,R,S  B) P,T,Q,R,S  C) T,P,R,Q,S  D) T,Q,P,R,S
**Answer: A) T,P,Q,R,S**

**Q6.** Kashif is older than Waqas but younger than Zain. Wahab is older than Zain. Who is the oldest?
A) Zain  B) Kashif  C) Wahab  D) Waqas
**Answer: C) Wahab** — order: Wahab > Zain > Kashif > Waqas
