## A. Squares, Cubes & Number Properties

**Q1.** What is 17²?
A) 279  B) 289  C) 299  D) 309
**Answer: B) 289**

**Q2.** What is 23²?
A) 519  B) 529  C) 539  D) 549
**Answer: B) 529**

**Q3.** What is the cube of 6?
A) 196  B) 206  C) 216  D) 226
**Answer: C) 216**

**Q4.** Which of these is a perfect square?
A) 144  B) 150  C) 160  D) 170
**Answer: A) 144** (= 12²)

**Q5.** Which of these is a perfect cube?
A) 100  B) 125  C) 150  D) 175
**Answer: B) 125** (= 5³)

**Q6.** Find the square root of 361.
A) 17  B) 18  C) 19  D) 20
**Answer: C) 19** (19² = 361)

**Q7.** Find the cube root of 1331.
A) 9  B) 10  C) 11  D) 12
**Answer: C) 11** (11³ = 1331)

**Q8.** What is 15² − 10²?
A) 115  B) 125  C) 135  D) 145
**Answer: B) 125** — using (15−10)(15+10) = 5×25 = 125

**Q9.** What is 9² + 12²?
A) 205  B) 215  C) 225  D) 235
**Answer: C) 225** — 81+144 = 225 (and note 225 = 15², a 9-12-15 right triangle)

**Q10.** How many perfect squares lie between 1 and 100 (inclusive)?
A) 8  B) 9  C) 10  D) 11
**Answer: C) 10** — 1,4,9,16,25,36,49,64,81,100

## B. Divisibility, LCM & HCF

**Q1.** Which number is divisible by both 3 and 4?
A) 18  B) 20  C) 24  D) 28
**Answer: C) 24**

**Q2.** What is the HCF of 12 and 18?
A) 3  B) 4  C) 6  D) 9
**Answer: C) 6**

**Q3.** What is the LCM of 4 and 6?
A) 10  B) 12  C) 18  D) 24
**Answer: B) 12**

**Q4.** Which of the following is a prime number?
A) 21  B) 29  C) 33  D) 39
**Answer: B) 29**

**Q5.** What is the LCM of 8 and 12?
A) 16  B) 20  C) 24  D) 32
**Answer: C) 24**

**Q6.** A number is divisible by 9 if its digit sum is divisible by 9. Which of these is divisible by 9?
A) 4562  B) 4563  C) 4564  D) 4565
**Answer: B) 4563** — digits 4+5+6+3=18, divisible by 9

**Q7.** The HCF of two numbers is 6 and their LCM is 36. One number is 12. What is the other?
A) 14  B) 16  C) 18  D) 20
**Answer: C) 18** — HCF × LCM = product of the two numbers → 6×36 = 216 = 12 × x → x = 18

## D. Ratio & Proportion

**Q1.** Divide 60 in the ratio 2:3. What is the smaller part?
A) 20  B) 22  C) 24  D) 26
**Answer: C) 24** — 5 parts = 60, 1 part = 12, smaller share = 2×12 = 24

**Q2.** If a:b = 2:3 and b:c = 4:5, find a:c.
A) 2:5  B) 8:15  C) 3:5  D) 4:5
**Answer: B) 8:15** — scale a:b to 8:12 (so b matches 12 in b:c=12:15) → a:c = 8:15

**Q3.** The ratio of boys to girls in a class is 3:2. There are 18 boys. How many girls are there?
A) 10  B) 12  C) 14  D) 16
**Answer: B) 12** — 3 parts = 18 → 1 part = 6 → girls = 2×6 = 12

**Q4.** Two cars cover the same distance. Their speeds are in ratio 5:7. What is the ratio of time they take?
A) 5:7  B) 7:5  C) 2:5  D) 1:1
**Answer: B) 7:5** — time is inversely proportional to speed for the same distance

## E. Averages

**Q1.** Find the average of 10, 20, 30, 40, 50.
A) 25  B) 28  C) 30  D) 32
**Answer: C) 30**

**Q2.** The average of 4 numbers is 20. After removing one number, the average of the remaining 3 is 18. What was the removed number?
A) 22  B) 24  C) 26  D) 28
**Answer: C) 26** — total of 4 = 80, total of 3 = 54, removed = 80−54 = 26

**Q3.** The average age of 5 students is 14 years. A teacher aged 32 joins the group. What is the new average age?
A) 15  B) 16  C) 17  D) 18
**Answer: C) 17** — students total = 70, +32 = 102, ÷6 = 17

## G. Simple & Compound Interest

**Q1.** Find the simple interest on Rs.1000 at 10% per annum for 2 years.
A) 150  B) 180  C) 200  D) 220
**Answer: C) 200** — SI = (P×R×T)/100 = (1000×10×2)/100 = 200

**Q2.** Find the compound interest on Rs.1000 at 10% per annum for 2 years (compounded yearly).
A) 200  B) 205  C) 210  D) 215
**Answer: C) 210** — Amount = 1000×1.1×1.1 = 1210, CI = 1210−1000 = 210

**Q3.** What principal gives Rs.50 simple interest in 1 year at 5% per annum?
A) 800  B) 900  C) 1000  D) 1100
**Answer: C) 1000**

## H. Time, Speed, Distance & Time-Work

**Q1.** A car travels 180 km in 3 hours. What is its speed?
A) 50  B) 55  C) 60  D) 65
**Answer: C) 60 km/h**

**Q2.** A train travels at 80 km/h. How long will it take to cover 200 km?
A) 2 hrs  B) 2.5 hrs  C) 3 hrs  D) 3.5 hrs
**Answer: B) 2.5 hrs**

**Q3.** A can finish a job in 10 days; B can finish the same job in 15 days. Working together, how many days will they take?
A) 5  B) 6  C) 7  D) 8
**Answer: B) 6 days** — combined rate = 1/10+1/15 = 1/6 of the job per day → 6 days

**Q4.** Two trains move toward each other at 40 km/h and 60 km/h, starting 200 km apart. After how long do they meet?
A) 1.5 hrs  B) 2 hrs  C) 2.5 hrs  D) 3 hrs
**Answer: B) 2 hrs** — combined closing speed = 100 km/h, time = 200/100 = 2 hrs

## J. Basic Statistics & Probability (light — relevant since you're applying for Data Science)

These same concepts appear in much greater depth in the **NED University Prep** folder — if you study `01` and `02` there, it directly helps here too.

**Q1.** Find the mean of: 5, 7, 9, 11, 13
A) 7  B) 8  C) 9  D) 10
**Answer: C) 9**

**Q2.** Find the median of: 4, 8, 15, 16, 23, 42
A) 14  B) 15  C) 15.5  D) 16
**Answer: C) 15.5** — even count, average the two middle numbers (15 and 16)

**Q3.** Find the mode of: 2, 3, 3, 4, 5, 5, 5, 6
A) 3  B) 4  C) 5  D) 6
**Answer: C) 5** — appears most often (3 times)

**Q4.** A fair coin is tossed once. What is the probability of getting heads?
A) 1/4  B) 1/3  C) 1/2  D) 1
**Answer: C) 1/2**

**Q5.** A fair six-sided die is rolled once. What is the probability of getting a number greater than 4?
A) 1/6  B) 1/3  C) 1/2  D) 2/3
**Answer: B) 1/3** — favorable outcomes {5,6} = 2 out of 6 = 1/3
